# tokenization

Tokenization is the process of replacing sensitive data or rights with a non-sensitive, unique token that can be used as a reference or substitute, without revealing the original underlying value or information.[^1][^2][^6]

### Types of Tokenization

- **Data Security**: Protects sensitive information such as payment card data or personal identifiers by replacing them with random tokens, reducing risk if systems are breached.[^4][^7][^1]
- **Payment Systems**: Payment data like card numbers are replaced with tokens for transactions, securing customer data from exposure at merchants or third parties.[^3][^9]
- **Asset Tokenization**: Converts real-world or digital assets (property, stocks, intellectual property) into blockchain tokens, enabling easier transfer, fractional ownership, and improved liquidity.[^5][^6]


### How Tokenization Works

- The original data is collected and sent to a secure system, which creates a token using randomization, cryptographic functions, or algorithms.[^8][^3]
- The token is stored and used in place of the real data for future transactions or verifications.
- Mapping between token and original data is maintained in a secure "vault;" only authorized systems can reverse tokens back to real data.[^7][^1][^8]
- In some blockchain use cases, tokens themselves represent unique digital rights and are tracked securely on distributed ledgers.[^6][^5]


### Benefits and Use Cases

- **Security**: Even if tokens are stolen, the real sensitive data remains protected; tokens cannot be directly reversed into their original form without a secure mapping.[^1][^8]
- **Privacy**: Reduces risk of data leaks and fraud by limiting exposure of true identifiers.[^7][^8]
- **Efficiency \& Compliance**: Simplifies payment processing, reduces scope of regulatory audits (e.g., PCI DSS for card payments), and is often easier to integrate with legacy systems than encryption.[^3][^8]

In summary, tokenization is a versatile method used in data security, payments, and asset management to protect value and privacy by substituting sensitive information with unique, isolated tokens that cannot be used to reconstruct the original data without access to secure, controlled systems.[^6][^8][^1]
